History of Chemistry

Journey through time to explore how chemistry evolved from ancient practices to the modern science that shapes our world today.

⚗️

Ancient Chemistry & Alchemy

Pre-3000 BCE

Early Practical Chemistry

Ancient civilizations developed practical chemical processes including metalworking, pottery glazing, fermentation, and perfume making. Egyptians practiced embalming and glassmaking.

500 BCE - 300 CE

Greek Theories of Matter

Greek philosophers proposed early atomic theories. Empedocles established the concept of four elements (earth, air, fire, water), while Democritus proposed that all matter consists of indivisible atoms.

700 - 1400 CE

Islamic Alchemy

Islamic scholars advanced alchemical practices, developing distillation techniques and laboratory equipment. Jabir ibn Hayyan (Geber) is considered the father of early chemistry, introducing experimental methodology.

1300 - 1600 CE

European Alchemy

Alchemy spread through Europe, focusing on transmutation of base metals into gold and the search for the philosopher's stone. Paracelsus pioneered the medical application of chemicals.

🔬

The Birth of Modern Chemistry

1661

Boyle's "The Sceptical Chymist"

Robert Boyle published his seminal work, distinguishing chemistry from alchemy and proposing that matter consists of corpuscles of various types and sizes.

1772-1777

Discovery of Oxygen

Carl Wilhelm Scheele and Joseph Priestley independently discovered oxygen. Antoine Lavoisier recognized it as an element and explained combustion, overthrowing the phlogiston theory.

1789

Lavoisier's Elements

Antoine Lavoisier published the first modern list of chemical elements and established the law of conservation of mass, founding modern chemistry.

1803

Dalton's Atomic Theory

John Dalton proposed his atomic theory, stating that elements are composed of atoms and compounds form when atoms of different elements combine in simple ratios.

Key Figures in Chemical History

These pioneering scientists shaped the development of chemistry through their groundbreaking discoveries and theories.

Antoine Lavoisier

1743-1794

Known as the "Father of Modern Chemistry," he established the law of conservation of mass and developed the first comprehensive list of elements.

Dmitri Mendeleev

1834-1907

Created the periodic table of elements, which organized known elements and predicted properties of undiscovered ones.

Marie Curie

1867-1934

Pioneered research on radioactivity, discovered radium and polonium, and was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

Linus Pauling

1901-1994

Revolutionized our understanding of chemical bonds and molecular structure, winning Nobel Prizes in both Chemistry and Peace.

⚛️

Atomic Age & Modern Chemistry

1869

Periodic Table

Dmitri Mendeleev published his periodic table, organizing elements by atomic weight and predicting properties of missing elements.

1897

Discovery of the Electron

J.J. Thomson discovered the electron, proving that atoms are divisible and launching the field of subatomic physics.

1913

Bohr's Atomic Model

Niels Bohr proposed his model of the atom with electrons orbiting the nucleus in discrete energy levels, combining quantum theory with atomic structure.

1953

DNA Structure

James Watson and Francis Crick, with contributions from Rosalind Franklin, determined the double helix structure of DNA, launching molecular biology.

Major Chemical Discoveries

These groundbreaking discoveries transformed our understanding of matter and its interactions.

🧪

Synthetic Urea

Friedrich Wöhler's 1828 synthesis of urea from inorganic compounds challenged vitalism and established organic chemistry.

🔋

Electrochemical Principles

Humphry Davy and Michael Faraday established electrochemical principles, leading to the discovery of new elements.

🦠

Penicillin

Alexander Fleming's 1928 discovery of penicillin launched the antibiotic revolution in medicine.

🧬

PCR Technique

Kary Mullis developed the polymerase chain reaction in 1983, revolutionizing molecular biology and genetics.
